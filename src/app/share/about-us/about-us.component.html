About Us

ANLU is an ancestral practice of the Kom community in the North-West of Cameroon. It is a practice with which women acted to bring justice against transgressions of the social order. Thus, our library aims to be a space of encounter, knowledge sharing and learning that will contribute to questioning our individual and common relationship to history in order to make us proud African citizens.

Our objectives :
- To promote African thoughts and perspectives through the African cultural and intellectual heritage;
- to involve young people in the African intellectual debate on Africa, to decolonise the collective memory;
- to build with African youth a free African society independent of neo-colonialism.
